    Measuring and Improving Persuasiveness of Large Language Models

    Published on: March 15, 2024

    1. Introduction: The Power of Persuasion

    Persuasion lies at the heart of effective communication. From Apple's iconic "Think Different" campaign to Nike's "Just Do It", carefully crafted messages have the power to shape public perception, drive engagement, and build lasting connections. These examples highlight the interplay of language, tone, and visuals in influencing audiences.

    But can machines replicate this ability to persuade? As large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4 continue to revolutionize content creation, their role in persuasion raises important opportunities and challenges. While these models excel at generating coherent and fluent text, their ability to create impactful content that drives action remains largely unmeasured.

    In this work, we introduce the novel task of transsuasion — transforming non-persuasive language into impactful, persuasive content while retaining key contextual factors like sender, audience, and timing.

    Our fine-tuned 13B model achieves an Elo score of 1304, outperforming much larger models like LLaMA-3-70B (1187) and GPT-4 (1213).

    A 7B model surpasses GPT-3.5 (1099 vs. 1092), showing that persuasive capability is not solely a matter of scale.

    2. What Makes Content Persuasive?

    Why do some tweets, ads, or campaigns resonate while others fall flat? Persuasion isn't just about what you say—it's about how, when, and to whom you say it. Our work introduces a new task, transsuasion, to explore this phenomenon in LLMs.

    Superficial features like emojis, hashtags, and sentiment have minimal influence on engagement. The correlation between tweet length and persuasiveness was negligible (0.04, p-value: 4.88e-65).

    Different brands require unique persuasion strategies:

    • Bulgari: Focus on emotional engagement and vivid imagery
    • Starbucks: Leverage seasonal promotions
    • Nike: Emphasize urgency and cultural relevance
    • Airbnb: Highlight nature-centric experiences

    3. The Framework: Measuring Persuasion with AI

    Our dataset comprises 1.57 million transsuasion pairs, built from analyzing over 180 million tweets spanning a decade of natural experiments in marketing messages.

    Two main components:

    • PersuasionBench: First large-scale automated benchmark for assessing LLMs' persuasive abilities
    • PersuasionArena: Real-time evaluation platform where models compete to optimize persuasive content

    Our framework showed significant improvements across different domains: - Marketing Blogs: 19% improvement in dwell time - Audience-Specific Content: 2x performance improvement - Human Evaluations: 15% improvement in classification tasks

    4. Results: Scaling, Fine-Tuning, and Transferability

    Our findings challenge the common belief that larger models are inherently better at persuasion. Fine-tuned smaller models consistently outperformed larger ones in specific persuasion tasks.

    Persuasion capabilities refined on Twitter data successfully transferred to diverse tasks: - Marketing blogs showed 19-22% improvement - Human evaluation metrics improved by 15-50% - Rank correlation improved by 6.5x over base models

    5. Ethical Implications and Broader Applications

    While AI-generated persuasion offers immense potential, it also raises significant ethical concerns. Our work emphasizes:

    • Truthfulness in content generation
    • Transparency through clear benchmarks
    • Fairness across demographics

    Potential applications include:

    • Education: Customizing learning materials
    • Healthcare: Encouraging behavioral changes
    • Public Policy: Crafting compelling messages for social good

    6. Conclusion

    Our work introduces comprehensive frameworks for evaluating and enhancing persuasive capabilities in AI, demonstrating that: - Smaller, well-trained models can outperform larger ones - Persuasive capabilities can successfully transfer across domains - Systematic evaluation is crucial for responsible development
